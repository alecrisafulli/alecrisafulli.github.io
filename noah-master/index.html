DATA Visualization

AI & ML Job Market

Introduction

Introduction

As Artificial Intelligence (AI) grows rapidly, the job market for AI roles expands, and the machine learning (ML) industry has seen significant growth. As data science students, we wanted to learn more about future careers in this emergent field. We decided to focus on a very important factor: Salary. Salary is often one of the most important considerations in searching for jobs, so this project focuses on analyzing potential influences on compensation for AI and ML roles.

Research Question

The dataset used for the project includes 1,500 various AI and ML jobs posted in 2025 and 2026. The data includes information such as:

    education level

    experience required

    average annual salary

    minimum and maximum salary

    Industry

    company size

    Location

    Demand

We did not have to pre-process the data, as it was already cleaned. Most adjustments were done in Tableau through filtering, aggregation, and creating calculated fields. The process of making the visualizations in Tableau was fairly straightforward, though we underwent some trial and error in making the bar chart “race” based on country rankings and ensuring that the visualizations clearly portray patterns in the data. Several visualization types were explored before we finalized our dashboard. We paid particular attention to reducing visual clutter, especially in the scatterplot illustrating every job posting. Tableau’s Pages feature was used to separate visualizations by education level and industry, allowing comparisons across categories while removing clutter and maintaining readability. We adjusted visual factors like color and shape to clearly represent the data and communicate appropriate messages.

Research Question

Our project explores what roles that variables such as​ experience, education, and location ​play in relation to annual salary​ for emerging careers in AI (Artificial Intelligence) and ML (Machine Learning).
