AI & ML Job Market

Introduction

Introduction

As Artificial Intelligence (AI) grows rapidly, the job market for AI roles expands, and the machine learning (ML) industry has seen significant growth. As data science students, we wanted to learn more about future careers in this emergent field. We decided to focus on a very important factor: Salary. Salary is often one of the most important considerations in searching for jobs, so this project focuses on analyzing potential influences on compensation for AI and ML roles.

Research Question

The dataset used for the project includes 1,500 various AI and ML jobs posted in 2025 and 2026. The data includes information such as:

    education level

    experience required

    average annual salary

    minimum and maximum salary

    Industry

    company size

    Location

    Demand

We did not have to pre-process the data, as it was already cleaned. Most adjustments were done in Tableau through filtering, aggregation, and creating calculated fields.
We also used a Cost of Living Index dataset so that we could look at relative salary on the point map. We fixed the city variable so it could join properly.

The process of making the visualizations in Tableau was fairly straightforward, though we underwent some trial and error in making the bar chart “race” based on country rankings and ensuring that the visualizations clearly portray patterns in the data. Several visualization types were explored before we finalized our dashboard. We paid particular attention to reducing visual clutter, especially in the scatterplot illustrating every job posting. Tableau’s Pages feature was used to separate visualizations by education level and industry, allowing comparisons across categories while removing clutter and maintaining readability. We adjusted visual factors like color and shape to clearly represent the data and communicate appropriate messages.

AI Job Market
Cost of Living Index

The point map shows the relative annual salary and the average minimum salary for various cities. The relative annual salary was calculated as [Annual Salary Usd]/([Cost of Living Index]/100). The points on the map differ in color and size. The color represents the relative annual salary of the city, and the size showcases the minimum salary. It seems that India, Iran and China have the highest relative annual salaries. The U.S. is somewhere in the middle, and Europe, Japan, and Indonesia are on the lower end. European cities tend to have around the same average salary, but with higher minimum salaries. On the other hand, several U.S. cities demonstrate higher average salaries, but lower minimum salary.

These findings imply that salaries are significantly influenced by location. The chart suggests that living in a city where AI/ML jobs are more relevant may increase salary. It also shows that depending on where you live, the salary may be steadier than other places. This reinforces the importance of considering location when searching for AI and ML careers.

The scatterplot shows years of experience and salary for each education level, separated using the Tableau pages feature. Through these comparisons, the scatterplots show that years of experience has more of an impact on salary for roles requiring less education. As education increases, experience matters less. The trendlines on the scatterplots help illustrate this. The correlation between salary and experience is more positive for the bootcamp/self-taught, Associate’s, and Bachelor’s classifications compared to the Master’s and PhD groups. The PhD group indicates little to no correlation between salary and experience. This suggests that employers may place greater value on advanced education than accumulated experience for some AI roles.

This visualization directly addresses the project’s research question by demonstrating how both education and experience interact to influence salary levels. The findings indicate that while experience remains important, advanced degrees may reduce the salary advantage typically gained through additional years of work experience.

The horizontal bar chart answers how location relates to salary. It ranks countries by average annual salary, sorted by industry using the pages function in Tableau. Each page shows the rankings for one industry, like finance or government. This visualization shows that no single country ranks first in salary for all AI and ML careers, but different rankings vary based on industry. For example, the US ranks first in the automotive and consulting industries, but France ranks first in the education industry.

The US ranks first most often, followed by the UAE. This suggests that industry is an important factor when looking for jobs in various countries. For example, if you want to work in research, Switzerland, the US, or France could be promising places to search for careers based on salary alone. However, if you are interested in manufacturing, the UAE could offer you the highest salary. Additionally, cost of living is important to consider, as well.
